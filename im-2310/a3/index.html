Who are the Cocteau Twins?

"Cocteau Twins" was a Scottish alternative rock band formed in 1979 in Grangemouth, Scotland. The band consisted of vocalist Elizabeth Fraser, guitarist Robin Guthrie, and bassist Simon Raymonde. Cocteau Twins established themselves as influential figures in the realms of post-punk and dream pop, renowned for their ethereal soundscapes and the enchanting, angelic vocals of Elizabeth Fraser.

The song "Cherry Coloured Funk", written and recorded by Cocteau Twins. Their album "Heaven or Las Vegas" was released in 1990. The album is known to be one of their most successful works to date. "Cherry Coloured Funk" quickly became their most iconic song and a staple to all Cocteau Twins listeners. Cocteau Twins' musical style, characterized by ethereal and dreamy soundscapes.

Despite their disbandment in 1997, Cocteau Twins' legacy and influence on alternative music remain profound. "Cherry Coloured Funk” is still known for their remarkable talent and creativity ,and continues to be one of Cocteau Twins' most exceptional achievements.
